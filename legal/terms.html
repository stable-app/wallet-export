These terms govern your use of the Stable app. By creating an account you agree to them. The plain-English summary sits beside each section — the short version: your money is yours, self-custodied; Stable provides software, not a bank account.

1. What Stable is (and isn't)

  • Stable is software for holding and moving USDC, a fully collateralized dollar stablecoin, using self-custodial wallets that only you control.
  • Stable is not a bank. Balances are not bank deposits and are not FDIC-insured. Your balance is USDC held at blockchain addresses controlled by your keys.
  • Stable never takes custody of your funds and cannot move, freeze, or recover them. Spending always requires authorization from your device.

2. Your account

  • You sign in with your email. Keep access to that inbox — it's how your account is secured.
  • You must be at least 18 (or the age of majority where you live) and use Stable only for yourself, not on behalf of others.
  • At launch, Stable is available to United States residents only. We may expand to other regions later.
  • One account per person. Usernames are first-come, first-served and may be reclaimed if impersonating someone.

3. Self-custody & your recovery key

  • Your wallet's private key is generated and secured so that only you can use it. You can export your recovery key at any time in Settings → Security.
  • If you share your recovery key, anyone holding it can spend your money. Stable will never ask for it.
  • If you lose access to your email and haven't exported your key, your funds may be unrecoverable. That is the nature of self-custody.

4. Payments are irreversible

  • Blockchain payments settle in seconds and cannot be reversed by Stable. Double-check usernames, addresses, and networks before sending.
  • Money sent to the wrong address, an unsupported token, or the wrong network may be permanently lost.

5. Fees

  • Sending to other Stable users is free. Fees for cross-chain transfers are shown in the app before you confirm — what you see is what you pay.
  • Fees may change; the app always displays the current fee before you commit.
  • Cross-chain transfers carry a flat fee paid to Stable — currently $1, or $2 when Ethereum is involved. The fee is included in the transaction you sign and goes to a fee wallet Stable controls. It is our service fee for delivering the transfer, not a charge collected by the blockchain.
  • When USDC arrives at one of your deposit addresses and is moved to your main balance (see Section 6), the same kind of flat fee is included in that transfer. It reimburses the network (gas) costs Stable pays to move the money, and it likewise goes to a Stable-controlled fee wallet.

6. Automatic transfers from your deposit addresses

  • Your account includes deposit addresses on Ethereum and other supported networks. By sending USDC to one of them, you instruct the app to automatically move ("sweep") those funds to your Solana wallet so they appear in your balance.
  • These transfers are a standing instruction from you: each one is signed by your own key, on your device, the next time you open the app. Stable cannot move the funds itself — the app only carries out the transfer you authorized by depositing.
  • The flat fee described in Section 5 is included in each of these transfers.

7. USDC and Circle

  • The dollars in your balance are USDC, a stablecoin issued by Circle — not by Stable. Circle's own terms govern USDC itself.
  • Like every USDC holder, you are exposed to the issuer: under legal process (such as a court order or a sanctions requirement), Circle can freeze or block USDC at the token level, including in self-custodial wallets.
  • That power belongs to Circle alone. Stable holds no keys and cannot move, freeze, or seize your funds — the most we can ever do is decline to provide service.

Don't attempt to disrupt the service, probe its infrastructure, or access other users' accounts. We may suspend app access (never your funds — we can't touch those) for violations.

9. Sanctions & compliance

  • U.S. sanctions law applies to Stable and to everyone who uses it. We screen the wallet addresses involved in transactions against government sanctions lists, and we check that you're using the app from a supported region.
  • Stable pays the network costs of your transactions. If screening flags a problem, or the law requires it, we may withhold that sponsorship and refuse to process transactions or provide service.
  • Refusing service never touches your money. Your funds remain fully yours: you can export your recovery key at any time and keep using your wallet with other software, without Stable.

12. Deleting your account

  • Some records survive deletion: on-chain history is public and permanent by the nature of blockchains; compliance and screening logs are kept for 10 years, as sanctions rules require; and payments involving other users remain in those users' transaction history (it's their record too), with your side anonymized.
  • Your wallet is yours regardless. Export your recovery key before deleting to keep full access to your funds without Stable.
